The Tutle and the Rabbit

June 1, 2016 / Brian Lusina

This has to be one of the oldest stories ever told. Think about it. It is about a smart wise turtle who beats the quick witty rabbit to the end of the finish line because the turtle plays to its strengths and banks on the rabbits haughty nature to win the race. It is meant to teach children and adults alike that being proud of your strengths and knowing them fully does not mean you will always win the race. Occasionally you will meet a challenge that will find a way to beat you even when it looks like you are the clear winner. Pretty simple right? What if the story was turned on its head? Instead of the underdog turtle winning, the rabbit takes the day. Would the moral still be learnt? Or will we just accept that the apparent strongest will always win the race?...
