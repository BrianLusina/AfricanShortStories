The Chameleon and the Mantis

May 28, 2016 / Brian Lusina

The chameleon was at it again. Blending in was always its skill. Insects never saw it coming. One minute it is living its short life buzzing here and there, the next it is trying to fight the clutches of a sticky tongue. They always fall for it. Even despite knowing what kills them, they still hang around. I suppose it is what mother nature intended so that life can be sustained. This was not the only sneaky trait of the chameleon. It has eyes that allow a sort of 360 view, this coupled with its firm grip, it was clearly a formidable prey for insects. But its sluggish movement was just something nature had to give it just to balance things out.


The praying mantis however was not as slow as the chameleon, it was quite fast and it killed with ferocity. It is slender, but it is not an enemy you want to encounter on its good day. Despite being in the seemingly small ecosystem together, these two rarely met. But nature had different plans
